Amazon Rainforest, Brazil

The Amazon Rainforest is one of the most biodiverse places on the planet. We spent a few days exploring the rivers and hiking through the jungle. One of the highlights was seeing a Three-toed Sloth hanging from a tree on a small island in the middle of the Rio Negro. Another was seeing a Pink River Dolphin, although it was so fast that we were not able to get a picture of it. We fished for Piranhas, and hiked through the jungle while our guide pointed out all of the different plants that are used for medicinal purposes. Some of the most interesting were a plant that is used to make a poison for the tips of blow darts, and another that is a natural mosquito repellent.
